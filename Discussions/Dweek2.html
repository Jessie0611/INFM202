Discussion Two

Brainstorming is a creative problem-solving method for conjuring up ideas. It can involve a team working together on a project or a single individual working on a solo project. Brainstorming encourages free thinking . Sketching is a visual method of communicating these ideas and concepts. It involves creating rough, informal drawings or diagrams to represent ideas, concepts, or designs.

Sketching can be done on paper or digitally, and it can range from simple doodles to more detailed drawings, depending on the purpose.

The choice between brainstorming and sketching depends on the specific goal and context. A combination of both methods can be more beneficial. Starting out with a brainstorming sessions to generate ideas and then use sketching to visualize and refine it. Personally, I find both methods valuable. I can be very indecisive at times so I think the combination works best for me. I also personally lack the gifted ability to create good art by hand - be it on paper or virtually - but a rough sketch can give me the beginnings of the visualization I need to design what I want with image editing software. So overall I would argue that both are needed. Brainstorming is excellent for stimulating creative thinking, while sketching provides a tangible way to convey those ideas.
